Visual Insights from a Decade of Citi Bikes in New York

By Joric Barber, Franco Magalhaes, Martha Njuguna and Margot Stern

Spring 2024

Since its 2013 launch, Citi Bike has transformed navigation in the largest city in the U.S., shaping riders' most frequented paths.

These are the paths riders have most often taken over the years.

2013

Citi Bike launches in late May with 330 stations in Manhattan south of 59th Street and Brooklyn north of Atlantic Avenue and west of Nostrand Avenue, recording 500,000 rides in its first month.

2014

In October, Citi Bike’s new owner announces a plan to double the fleet from 6,000 to 12,000 bikes and expand the program.

2015

Citi Bike expands with 140 new stations in Bed-Stuy, Greenpoint, Long Island City, Williamsburg, and the Upper East and West Sides, while riders achieve a milestone of 10 million trips in one year.

2016

Citi Bike expands with 140 new stations in Manhattan, Jersey City, and Brooklyn, increasing its fleet from 8,000 to 10,000 bikes and reaching its 100,000th annual member.

2017

Citi Bike adds 142 stations in Harlem, East Harlem, Astoria, Crown Heights, and Prospect Heights, expanding its fleet from 10,000 to 12,000 bikes.

2018

NYC and Lyft agree to a $100 million investment to double Citi Bike’s service area and triple its fleet to 40,000 bikes in five years, while expanding the Reduced Fare Bike Share program for NYCHA residents and SNAP recipients.

2019

Since its 2013 debut, Citi Bike's popularity has soared, doubling its fleet and tripling daily rides, now available on the Lyft app, but security concerns with brakes and batteries have led to the removal of 2,500 e-bikes from its fleets in NYC, San Francisco, and Washington DC.

2020

Citi Bike reintroduces e-bikes in NYC, noting new COVID-19 ridership patterns with evening commutes more than doubling compared to mornings.

2021

Citibike completes its Manhattan expansion by extending to Inwood and Washington Heights, launches in Hoboken to create a seamless bikeshare system with Jersey City and NYC, and faces more complicated and unpredictable riding patterns due to hybrid work schedules, school reopenings, and warm weather recreational rides.

2022

In May, Citibike introduced powerful new e-bikes with enhanced safety features, expanding to 23,500 bikes (4,500 e-bikes) across 1,500+ stations, extending service to Astoria, Sunset Park, Inwood, and the West Bronx, and removing the $3 price cap for members riding e-bikes between Queens and Brooklyn.

2023

Citibike, with over 30,000 bikes and 2,000 stations, plans to have ebikes make up half its fleet by 2024, and despite their popularity in NYC and appearances in shows like Succession, Blue Bloods, and Entergalactic, the agreement with NYC lacks system expansion or public funding.

2024

Citibike is raising prices to fund station upgrades and more e-bikes, with plans to open its first electrified stations later this year to streamline e-bike charging and reduce manual battery swaps by 80 percent.
